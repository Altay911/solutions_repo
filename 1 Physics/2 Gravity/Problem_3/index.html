Trajectories of Freely Released Payloads Near Earth

Fundamental Orbital Mechanics

Classification of Trajectories

  1. Circular Orbit:
  2. Perfect balance between velocity and gravity
  3. Constant altitude and speed

  4. Required velocity: ~7.8 km/s at 200 km altitude

  5. Elliptical Orbit:

  6. Intermediate energy between circular and escape
  7. Perigee and apogee distances vary

  8. Velocity between first and second cosmic velocities

  9. Parabolic Trajectory:

  10. Exact escape velocity achieved
  11. Open-ended orbit (escape path)

  12. Velocity equals second cosmic velocity (~11.2 km/s at surface)

  13. Hyperbolic Trajectory:

  14. Exceeds escape velocity
  15. Excess kinetic energy results in hyperbolic excess velocity
  16. Used for interplanetary missions

Key Parameters

Determining Factors

Parameter Effect on Trajectory
Initial Velocity Determines orbit shape
Release Altitude Affects gravitational strength
Flight Path Angle Influences orbit eccentricity
Atmospheric Drag Causes orbital decay (LEO)

Critical Velocity Thresholds

  1. Suborbital:
  2. Velocity < 7.8 km/s

  3. Ballistic arc returning to Earth

  4. Orbital:

  5. 7.8 km/s < v < 11.2 km/s

  6. Closed elliptical or circular orbit

  7. Escape:

  8. v ≥ 11.2 km/s
  9. Parabolic or hyperbolic departure
